Essential classical Christian doctrines

Classical Christianity, like any group, has a basic set of non-negotiable beliefs or doctrines held to be objectively true for all people at all times.

The non-negotiable essential doctrines of classical Christianity are:

  1. Final authority of scripture - The Bible is the final standard of measure (i.e. canon) in all things. Inspired by God (2 Timothy 3:16), written down by humans. It is the final authority and without it nothing can be affirmed or asserted with confidence.
  2. The bodily resurrection of Jesus - Jesus was crucified to death and return from death alive again. On this single event hangs all of Christianity. If he did not return from death, then Christianity is worthless (1 Thessalonians 4:13-18, John 2:19-21).
  3. Jesus is God - John 8:58 with Exodus 3:14. Also, John 1:1, 14; 10:30-33, Col 2:9, Philippians 2:5-8
  4. Monotheism - God has no equal. There is only one uncasued, first cause, supreme being, and creator. Isaiah 44:6
  5. Original Sin - Humans are fallen and culpable. Sin renders everyone guilty before God. (Romans 3:23)
  6. Salvation by grace alone through trust in Jesus alone - Christ alone served the guilty sentence for human sin. Christians trust in Jesus alone to save them from an eternal separation from God due to human sinfulness (Romans 11:6, John 6, Eph. 2:8-9)

These essentials are not an argument for Classical Christianity. That is a separate topic. The purpose of listing these essentials beliefs is to clarify the non-negotiable fundamental beliefs of classical Christianity.

Additionally, these fundamental beliefs are in contradiction to Mormonism, Jehovah’s Witness, Atheism, Deism, Islamism, Buddhism, Judaism, and Hinduism all teach as true. If any of other religion is objectively true, then Christianity is false. And, if Christianity is objectively true then Mormonism, Jehovah’s Witness, Atheism, Deism, Islamism, Buddhism, Judaism, and Hinduism are all false. Logically they could all be false, but what can’t be true, is that they are all true.
